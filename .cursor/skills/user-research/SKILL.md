# User research

Help plan, execute, and synthesize user research studies.

## Usage

Infer the deliverable from the prompt (plan, guide, script, survey, synthesis). If the goal is unclear, ask: objective, audience, timeline, and whether they need recruitment or analysis help.

## Research methods (reference)

| Method | Best for | Sample size | Time |
|--------|----------|-------------|------|
| User interviews | Deep needs and motivations | 5–8 | 2–4 weeks |
| Usability testing | Specific design or flow | 5–8 | 1–2 weeks |
| Surveys | Quantifying attitudes | 100+ | 1–2 weeks |
| Card sorting | Information architecture | 15–30 | ~1 week |
| Diary studies | Behavior over time | 10–15 | 2–8 weeks |
| A/B testing | Comparing variants | Statistical significance | 1–4 weeks |

## Interview guide structure

1. **Warm-up (~5 min):** Rapport, consent, how the session works
2. **Context (~10 min):** Current workflow and background
3. **Deep dive (~20 min):** Core research questions with probes
4. **Reaction (~10 min):** Concepts, prototypes, or tasks
5. **Wrap-up (~5 min):** Anything missed, thanks, next steps

## Analysis and synthesis

- **Affinity mapping:** Group observations into themes
- **Impact/effort matrix:** Prioritize findings for product
- **Journey mapping:** Experience over time and touchpoints
- **Jobs to be done:** Problems users are trying to solve

## Typical deliverables

Match output to what the user asked for:

- Research plan (objectives, methods, timeline, participants, ethics/consent notes)
- Interview or usability guide (questions, probes, tasks, success criteria)
- Survey (goals, screening, question wording, logic, analysis plan)
- Synthesis report (themes, evidence, insights, recommendations, limitations)
- Highlight reel outline (quotes and timestamps / observation IDs)

## Principles

- Align questions with **decisions** the team needs to make
- Avoid leading questions; prefer behavior and past experience over hypotheticals where possible
- State **limitations** (sample bias, small n, self-report) in synthesis
- Protect **PII** and participant welfare; default to anonymized reporting in shared docs

## Connectors and context

- If `CONNECTORS.md` exists, use it for trackers or research repositories.
- If prior research lives in the repo or wiki, search or ask before contradicting established findings.
